Open Positions for Postdoctoral Associate

A Postdoctoral Research Associate position is available in Biomedical Optical Imaging Laboratory in the Departments of Medicine and Bioengineering at University of Pittsburgh, which is one of the nation's top ranked schools in biomedical research. We look for an innovative and highly motivated individual to work on various aspects of advanced microscopy and image analysis, including instrument development, image processing, basic biology and clinical applications. Our technical development includes high-throughput super-resolution microscopy, low-cost optical devices, 3D live-cell imaging and related machine-learning based image processing and disease classification. We aim to understand the pathophysiology of normal biological processes and diseases (such as cancer, inflammatory bowel disease, pancreatic diseases and aging). We work primarily with patient-derived samples with the ultimate goal to improve patient care.

The candidate is expected to independently conduct research, publish scientific findings in prestigious journals and deliver verbal technical presentations in international conferences. The candidate will have a unique great opportunity to work in a multidisciplinary team that encompasses expertise in bioengineering, electrical engineering, cancer biology, pathology, and oncology at University of Pittsburgh, University of Pittsburgh Medical Center (UPMC) and other top medical centers around the country. Outstanding individuals will also have the opportunity to advance to research faculty positions in the future.

We are interested in candidates with a Ph.D. in biomedical engineering, electrical engineering, optics, biophysics or a related biomedical field. The candidate must be self-motivated with a strong passion for research. A strong background in optical imaging techniques, quantitative image analysis and programming skills (e.g., Matlab, Python) are required. The expertise in at least one of the following areas is highly desirable: optical microscopy, image processing, super-resolution imaging and biophysics. Hands- on experience on using or developing super-resolution microscopy and fluorescence staining, or hardware programming is highly desirable. We are also interested in motivated candidates with a Ph.D. in biomedical fields with solid skills in molecular biology and genomic techniques and sufficient background to quickly learn technical aspects of high-resolution imaging (such as programming and quantitative analysis). We offer excellent salary and benefits. For interested candidates, please send your CV to Prof. Yang Liu by email (liuy@pitt.edu).
